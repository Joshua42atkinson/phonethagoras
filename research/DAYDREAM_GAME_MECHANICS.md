# DAYDREAM — Game Mechanics Design

> The player is the AUTHOR. The character is the HERO.
> Game mechanics don't punish the player — they create drama for the character.
> The author wields Focus, Synergy, and Consequence as story-crafting tools.

---

## Philosophy: Two Layers

There are TWO entities in every session:

### Layer 1: The Player (Author)
- **Free.** No punishment, no failure, no wrong moves.
- **Creative.** Fills word-slots, chooses branches, builds the story.
- **Reflective.** Sees the Archetype mirror at the end — "this is how you create."
- Like a Dungeon Master, game designer, or Minecraft builder.

### Layer 2: The Character (Hero)
- **Governed by game mechanics.** Has Focus, faces challenges, earns Resonance.
- **Experiences consequences.** Running out of Focus means the hero struggles. Synergies mean the hero has a breakthrough.
- **Lives the Hero's Journey.** The character encounters, stumbles, grows, transforms.
- Like a protagonist in a novel the player is writing.

### How They Interact
```
PLAYER decides:  "I'll place RESILIENCE in the Action slot."
GAME calculates: "That costs 2 Focus. The character now has 3 Focus left."
STORY generates: "The hero grits her teeth and pushes across the bridge.
                  She can feel her strength draining, but she doesn't stop."
PLAYER sees:     The consequence is IN THE STORY, not on the player.
                 The player isn't punished — the hero is challenged.
```

The mechanics are **narrative tools**:

| Mechanic | Player Experience | Character Experience |
|----------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Focus depletion** | "I'm crafting tension" | "The hero is exhausted" |
| **Synergy/Resonance** | "I connected two ideas" | "The hero has a breakthrough" |
| **Challenge word** | "This is the climax I'm building toward" | "The hero faces her greatest test" |
| **Recycler return** | "This word came back richer" | "The hero recognizes an old friend" |
| **Wrong slot placement** | "I'm creating conflict" | "The words don't fit — something is off" |

Like Minecraft: there is no way to "lose" at building. But within your creation, characters can struggle, grow, and triumph — and THAT is what makes the story worth telling.

## The Core Loop: Story Building

```
Each turn, the story presents a MOMENT with word-slots:

  "In the _______ , a _______ begins to _______ ."
       [SETTING]      [SUBJECT]        [ACTION]

The player fills the slots from their hand:

  "In the [MIRROR CHAMBER], a [REFLECTION] begins to [SPEAK]."

Different cards create different stories:

  "In the [GARDEN], a [MEMORY] begins to [GROW]."

Same structure. Different meaning. Both are "right."
```

**The VAAM insight:** There IS no "right" word — meaning comes from the combination. The student discovers this by playing.

## The Hero's Journey as Chapter Structure

Joseph Campbell's monomyth gives every curriculum a natural story arc. The 12 stages map directly to how a student encounters new vocabulary:

### The 12 Stages → 6 Chapters

| Chapter | Hero's Journey Stages | Curriculum Purpose | Word Types |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **1. The Ordinary World** | Ordinary World + Call to Adventure | What do you already know? Familiar ground. | Hero ★ — common, grounding words |
| **2. Crossing the Threshold** | Refusal + Meeting Mentor + Crossing | First real challenge. Words that surprise. | Hero ★ + Outlaw ★★ — new territory |
| **3. Tests and Allies** | Tests, Allies, Enemies | Building relationships between words. Synergy discovery. | Outlaw ★★ — words that connect |
| **4. The Cave** | Approach + Ordeal | The hardest words. Metacognitive depth. | EdgeLord ★★★ — abstract concepts |
| **5. The Transformation** | Reward + Road Back | Synthesis. Using words in new contexts. | BestSelf ★★★★ — integration |
| **6. The Return** | Resurrection + Return with Elixir | The story you built IS the treasure. Trail review. | All — your SpellBook |

### How Chapters Feel

**Chapter 1 — "The Ordinary World"**
> The story opens gently. The slots are easy to fill.
> "In the [STILLNESS], a [PRESENCE] begins to [BREATHE]."
> The player learns the mechanic without pressure.

**Chapter 4 — "The Cave"**
> The slots ask harder questions. Abstract words appear.
> "In the [UNCERTAINTY], a [BIAS] begins to [DISTORT]."
> The player must think about what fits — not because there's a penalty,
> but because they CARE about their story making sense.

**Chapter 6 — "The Return"**
> The player reads back the story they built. Every word they placed
> is there. The trail review shows their journey. The SpellBook
> shows what they discovered. The story is THEIRS.

## The Crafting Table: Story Slots

Each story moment has **3 slots** that the player fills with word-cards. The slots map to grammar roles, teaching parts of speech through building:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                              │
│  ┌─────────┐  ┌─────────┐  ┌─────────┐     │
│  │ SETTING │  │ SUBJECT │  │ ACTION  │     │
│  │  ◆ noun │  │ ☆ term  │  │ ◇ verb  │     │
│  │ [empty] │  │ [empty] │  │ [empty] │     │
│  └─────────┘  └─────────┘  └─────────┘     │
│                                              │
│  Story: "..."                                │
│  (fills in as you place cards)               │
│                                              │
│  ┌────┐  ┌────┐  ┌────┐                     │
│  │PATI│  │CLAR│  │BIAS│  ← your hand        │
│  └────┘  └────┘  └────┘                     │
│                                              │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Placing a Card
- Drag a card from your hand to a slot
- The story text GENERATES based on what's in all 3 slots
- Placing a card is always valid — any word can inspire any slot
- Some combinations create **Resonance** (synergy) — a special glow + bonus story text

### The ○ Void (Abstract) Wild Card
Abstract concept cards (○ Void symbol) can be placed in ANY slot. They're versatile but unpredictable — the story generated might surprise you. This teaches that abstract concepts are flexible and context-dependent.

### Modifiers (△ Prism)
Adjective/adverb cards don't take a slot — they **attach** to an existing card, modifying it. Placing "Resilient" (△) onto "Presence" (◆) creates "Resilient Presence" — a compound concept that generates richer story text.

## Character Focus: The Hero's Drama

While the player (Author) has absolute freedom to place any card at any time without penalty, the **Hero** in the story operates with finite strength, tracked as **Focus**. Focus represents the character's cognitive energy, willpower, and physical stamina within the narrative.

### How Focus Works:
- **Card Costs:** Placing a card in a story slot costs the Hero Focus based on the word's Mastery Tier (e.g., common words cost 1 Focus, while abstract *EdgeLord* words cost 3 Focus).
- **Dynamic Exhaustion:** As the Hero's Focus drains, the generated story text reflects their struggle (e.g., description of heavier breathing, faltering steps, or mental fatigue).
- **Crisis Gating:** If the Hero's Focus reaches `0`, the current story branch locks. The player must guide the Hero through a somatic rest point (matching a deep, calming frequency in the pitch gate) to restore Focus and unlock the next path.
- **Narrative Stakes:** Low-Focus states unlock unique branch choices—often filled with emotional depth or risky options—allowing the player to craft a high-stakes, dramatic storyline.

## Branching: Choose Your Own Adventure

After filling the slots and reading the generated story, the player faces a **branch point**:

```
Your story so far:
  "In the mirror chamber, your reflection begins to speak —
   but the words aren't yours."

What do you do?

  → [LISTEN]     "Stay and hear what it says."
  → [CHALLENGE]  "Ask: whose words are these?"
  → [LEAVE]      "Walk away. Some mirrors lie."
```

### How Branches Work
- Each branch leads to a **different next moment** with new slots to fill
- The branches are generated from the curriculum DAG edges
- **Swipe right** = first branch, **swipe left** = second, **swipe down** = third
- There is no "wrong" branch — each explores different words
- Your choices determine which words you encounter and in what order

### Branches Create Unique Journeys
```
Student A: Listen → Mirror → Reflect → Clarity (Mind-heavy path)
Student B: Challenge → Bias → Self-awareness → Agency (Action-heavy path)
Student C: Leave → Patience → Presence → Resilience (Body-heavy path)
```

All three students encounter the same vocabulary — but in different orders, through different stories, with different emotional contexts. Same curriculum. Three completely different experiences.

## Resonance (Not Synergy)

When cards in your story naturally relate to each other, they **Resonate**. Resonance isn't a reward or a score — it's a recognition that you built something meaningful.

### How Resonance Works
- Two cards with synergy links are placed in the same story moment
- The slot borders glow with a warm pulse
- Bonus story text appears: "These words echo each other..."
- A small **Resonance Thread** is recorded in your SpellBook

### Why It Matters (Not for Points)
Resonance threads become visible in the trail review at the end:

> "In your journey, 'Patience' and 'Resilience' resonated 3 times.
> These words are woven together in your understanding.
> You see them as connected — and they are."

This is the **AI Mirror**: reflecting the student's meaning-making back to them without judgment. Not "good job" or "try harder" — just "this is what you built."

## The SpellBook: Your Creation

The SpellBook isn't a collection to optimize. It's a **journal of your journey**.

### What's In It
| Element | What It Records |
|---------|----------------|
| **Words encountered** | Every vocabulary word you met |
| **Stories created** | The story text generated from your card placements |
| **Resonance threads** | Which words you connected together |
| **Branches chosen** | Your path through the Hero's Journey |
| **Card art evolution** | Art that grew as you revisited words (4 mastery tiers) |

### Mastery as Familiarity
Mastery levels aren't achievements — they're reflections of familiarity:

| Level | Name | What It Means |
|-------|------|---------------|
| 🔮 | Encountered | "You've seen this word." |
| ⚡ | Experienced | "You've built a story with this word." |
| 🌟 | Owned | "This word keeps appearing in your stories." |
| 👑 | Mastered | "This word resonates with others. It's part of your vocabulary." |

Words advance naturally through replay. No grinding, no quizzes. If a word keeps appearing in your stories (because the Recycler brings it back and you keep placing it), it becomes part of you.

## The Recycler: Words Come Back

Played cards don't disappear — they enter **The Recycler** and return later, evolved.

```
First encounter:  "Patience" — 🔮 sketch art, simple story
Second encounter: "Patience" — ⚡ watercolor art, richer story text  
Third encounter:  "Patience" — 🌟 detailed art, your previous stories referenced
Fourth encounter: "Patience" — 👑 legendary art, full resonance history shown
```

The Recycler IS spaced repetition — but the student experiences it as "this word came back, and now I have a deeper story to tell with it."

No forced review. No flashcard drills. The word returns because the story needs it.

## Archetypes: Your Story Tells You Who You Are

As the student plays across multiple curricula, patterns emerge. The game reflects these patterns as an **Archetype** — not a label, a mirror.

| Archetype | Pattern | What the Game Says |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Oracle** | Places Mind words in Subject slots often | "You look for meaning behind meaning." |
| **Bard** | Creates the most Resonance threads | "You weave words together like songs." |
| **Cultivator** | Replays words many times (deep mastery) | "You tend your garden patiently." |
| **Templar** | Explores every branch (breadth over depth) | "You walk every path to see the whole map." |
| **Architect** | Balanced placement across all channels | "You build from every direction." |
| **Hermeticist** | Always chooses "dig deeper" at branches | "You see the code behind the words." |

The archetype doesn't change gameplay. It doesn't give buffs. It's purely reflective — "this is how you create." The student can look at it and think about whether that's who they want to be, or whether they want to explore differently next time.

## The Full Experience (One Session)

```
SESSION: "Bias & Mirrors" — Chapter 1: The Ordinary World

1. OPENING
   "You stand at the edge of a forest of glass trees.
    The path ahead has three clearings."
   
   Your hand: [Presence] [Patience] [Bias]

2. FIRST MOMENT — fill the slots
   "In the _______, a _______ begins to _______."
   
   Player places: [CLEARING] [PRESENCE] [BREATHE]
   → Story generates:
   "In the clearing, your presence begins to breathe
    with the rhythm of the glass leaves."

3. BRANCH POINT
   → [Walk deeper]  → [Sit and wait]  → [Touch a tree]

4. Player swipes RIGHT: "Walk deeper"
   New moment with new hand draws...

5. RESONANCE CHECK
   "Presence" and "Patience" are in the same moment
   → Warm glow. "These words echo each other."

6. ...continue for 8-12 moments...

7. CHAPTER END — The Return
   "You emerge from the glass forest.
    Here is the story you built:"
   
   [Trail review: your words, your branches, your resonances]
   [SpellBook updated with mastery levels]
   [Archetype whisper: "You walk like an Oracle..."]

8. SAVE
   SpellBook persists locally. Story saved as your creation.
```

## Implementation: ECS Additions

```rust
// Story slots — the crafting table
struct StoryMoment {
    setting: Option<Entity>,     // ◆ noun slot
    subject: Option<Entity>,     // ☆ term slot
    action: Option<Entity>,      // ◇ verb slot
    modifiers: Vec<Entity>,      // △ prism attachments
    generated_text: String,      // LLM/template output
}

// Hero's Journey chapter tracking
struct Chapter {
    name: String,
    journey_stage: JourneyStage, // OrdinaryWorld..ReturnWithElixir
    moments: Vec<StoryMoment>,   // moments the player built
    branches_taken: Vec<String>, // which paths they chose
}

enum JourneyStage {
    OrdinaryWorld,
    CallToAdventure,
    CrossingThreshold,
    TestsAndAllies,
    TheOrdeal,
    Transformation,
    TheReturn,
}

// Resonance (not synergy, not score)
struct ResonanceThread {
    word_a: String,
    word_b: String,
    times_resonated: u32,
    story_moments: Vec<String>,  // the stories where they appeared together
}

// Character Focus resource (governs drama, not player choice)
struct CharacterState {
    focus: u32,
    max_focus: u32,
    exhausted: bool,
}
```

## What Changed from v1

| v1 (Removed) | v2 (Replaced With) | Why |
|-------------|--------------------|----|
| Focus (player energy cost) | Focus as Character Resource | Shifted tension from player constraints to dramatic character consequences |
| Card costs by Stage | All cards equally playable | Complex words aren't "harder to use" |
| Synergy bonuses (+2) | Resonance (reflective glow, no score) | Bonuses create optimization, not creation |
| Archetype buffs | Archetype as mirror (no gameplay effect) | Buffs incentivize min-maxing |
| Challenge/boss words | Hero's Journey chapter climax | Bosses create anxiety, not wonder |
| Win conditions | The story you built IS the outcome | There is nothing to "win" |
